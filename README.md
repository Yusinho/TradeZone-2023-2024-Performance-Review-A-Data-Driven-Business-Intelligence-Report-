# TradeZone-2023-2024-Performance-Review-A-Data-Driven-Business-Intelligence-Report

I am excited to share a recent data analytics project I completed for TradeZone, a fast-growing Nigerian e-commerce platform operating across Lagos, Abuja, Kano, Port Harcourt, and Ibadan.
The task involved end-to-end data work across three stages:
Data Cleaning — standardised city names, normalised product categories, resolved NULL values, removed duplicate records, and validated order amounts against line-item totals across a real-world messy dataset.
SQL Analysis — wrote eight business queries covering customer acquisition and 30-day conversion rates, product revenue performance, seller fulfilment efficiency, quarterly revenue trends, customer spend segmentation, payment method preferences by state, review ratings vs sales performance, and top seller bonus qualification.
Analyst Memo — translated query results into a structured business memo with actionable recommendations for the Head of Growth and Head of Seller Operations.
Key Insights from the Data:
📌 Customer conversion within the first 30 days was significantly below 50% in several states — meaning TradeZone was spending to acquire customers and then losing them before a single purchase.
📌 Revenue was heavily concentrated in a narrow set of products and sellers — a single-point-of-failure that would be immediately visible if one or two top sellers reduced activity or exited the platform.
📌 Low-rated products (below 3.0) were still generating meaningful order volume at prices comparable to better-rated alternatives — a clear sign that the review system was not functioning as a market signal.
📌 The data could not explain why customers churned because there was no attribution or post-purchase feedback data — a gap I flagged as a priority data collection recommendation for 2025.
These were not just numbers on a screen. Each finding pointed to a real business decision the leadership team needed to make before the next planning cycle.
